Weaknesses of the DPR (Indonesian House of Representatives)

  • Corruption and Bribery: Many members caught in corruption scandals.
  • Low Productivity: Often focused on party politics, poor attendance.
  • High Salary: Large benefits without matching performance.
  • Conflicts: Internal political disputes instead of policymaking.
  • Lack of Transparency: Important decisions made without public input.
  • Poor Ethics: Scandals and inappropriate behavior damaging credibility.

Pros vs Cons of DPR

Pros Cons
Represents people in theory Often fails to truly represent citizens
Checks and balances on government Weak accountability due to corruption
Creates laws and policies Many laws lack transparency and public input
Platform for democracy More focused on party interests than national interests

Alternative Without DPR

If the DPR cannot reform, Indonesia could adopt new systems: more direct democracy, digital platforms for citizen input, or independent councils with transparent oversight. The people deserve institutions that truly serve their interests.
